Confidential Clinical Encounter Note — Office Visit

Patient Name:Maria Santos
Date of Birth:DOB 03/15/1985
MRN: MRN-2847561
Date of Service: 03/10/2026
Provider: Dr. Elena Reyes, MD
Encounter Type: Established Patient Office Visit
Location: provider office
Insurance: PPO plan through BlueCross, $20 PCP copay, no referral required

Subjective

Patient is a 39-year-old female who presents today as an established patient of this practice. She reports she was seen here eleven months ago for a routine annual wellness examination. She is generally in good health and has a history of type 2 diabetes mellitus, but it has been stable.

The patient presents today with a chief complaint of respiratory illness. She describes productive cough with yellowish sputum, persistent fever reaching as high as 102.4°F at home, generalized fatigue limiting her daily activities, and progressive shortness of breath over the past five days. She denies nausea, vomiting, chest pain, hemoptysis, or recent travel.

Review of systems is otherwise negative for rash, joint pain, urinary symptoms, or changes in vision or hearing.

Objective

General: The patient is alert, oriented, and appears mildly ill but is in no acute respiratory distress at rest.

Vital Signs: temperature 101.2°F, oxygen saturation of 95% on room air, respiratory rate of 22 breaths per minute, and auscultation revealing crackles over the right lower lobe. Heart rate 92, blood pressure 118/76 mmHg, weight 142 lbs. BMI 24.1.

HEENT: Oropharynx is mildly erythematous without exudates. Tympanic membranes are clear bilaterally. Nasal mucosa is mildly congested.

Cardiovascular: Regular rate and rhythm. Abdomen: Soft, non-tender, non-distended. Extremities: No peripheral edema.

Assessment

Based on the patient’s presenting symptoms, vital sign findings, and physical examination, the clinical impression is Pneumonia, unspecified organism. The combination is consistent with a lower respiratory tract infection affecting the right lower lobe.

Plan

1. Imaging: Order a portable chest X-ray to be completed at an outpatient radiology facility to confirm infiltrate and assess extent of involvement.

2. Antibiotic therapy: Initiate azithromycin 500 mg orally on day one, followed by 250 mg once daily for four additional days for treatment of community-acquired pneumonia.

Electronically signed: Dr. Elena Reyes, MD — NPI 1234567890 — 03/10/2026 at 11:42 AM
